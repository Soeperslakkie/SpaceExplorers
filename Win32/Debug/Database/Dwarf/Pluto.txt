Pluto is a dwarf planet located in the Kuiper Belt, a region beyond Neptune. It has a diameter of about 2,377 kilometers (1,480 miles) and is known for its complex and varied surface, including ice plains, mountain ranges, and a large heart-shaped region called Sputnik Planitia. Discovered in 1930, Pluto was reclassified from a planet to a dwarf planet in 2006 by the International Astronomical Union. It has five known moons, with Charon being the largest and closest.
